History

Early Beginnings of White Water Rafting - Ancient River Navigation: People have been navigating rivers by boat for thousands of years. - Modern White Water Rafting: The sport as we know it today traces back to the early 19th century in America. - First Recorded Attempt: In 1811, an attempt to navigate the Snake River in Wyoming was made but deemed too difficult and dangerous ¹ ² ³. Development of Commercial White Water Rafting - First Commercial Trip: The first recorded commercial white water rafting trip took place in 1940 on the Salmon River in Idaho. - Post-WWWII Growth: After World War II, surplus military rafts became available, leading to increased commercial rafting trips. - 1960s Popularity Surge: White water rafting gained popularity in the 1960s and '70s, with companies like All-Outdoors starting in California ¹ ⁴.

Evolution of Safety and Equipment - Safety Regulations: Commercial white water rafting is now heavily regulated with safety paramount. - Equipment Advancements: Modern rafts are made from materials like PVC, polyurethane, and Hypalon, improving durability and safety ¹ ⁵.
